Panic Disorder & Agoraphobia

What is it?

Panic disorder involves recurrent, unexpected panic attacks, which are sudden periods of intense fear or discomfort. Agoraphobia is often linked to panic disorder and refers to the fear of places or situations where escape might be difficult or where help may not be available during a panic attack.

Symptoms

  • Panic Attacks with chest pain, shortness of breath, dizziness or other physical symptoms
  • Fear of having another panic attack
  • Agoraphobia: Avoidance of places (crowded areas, open spaces or public transports)

Possible Treatment

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy:


  • Particularly exposure therapy to reduce fear of triggering situations.

Medications:


  • Antidepressants
  • Benzodiazepines (for short-term relief)

Lifestyle Changes:


  • For stress management and exercise 1
