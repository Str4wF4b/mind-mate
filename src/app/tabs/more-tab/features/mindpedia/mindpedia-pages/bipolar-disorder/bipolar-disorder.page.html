Bipolar Disorder

What is it?

A condition in which the mood alternates between mania and depression. This alternation often occurs for a lifetime. Mania is a change in mood that is persistent in intensity and duration and also occurs with symptoms such as the urge to talk or increased drive. People with bipolar disorder occasionally experience happiness, joy or pride. In these moments, those affected are inclined to spontaneously hug the other person, laugh, dance or talk to themselves.

Symptoms of Mania

  • Language: loud, urgent, vigorous, confrontational
  • Activity: increased, changing, restless, centre of attention
  • Posture: powerful, upright, restless, driven
  • Facial expression: lively, many facial expressions, exaggerated
  • Emotions: irritable, funny, exaggerated, carefree
  • Psychological-vegetative: little need for sleep, full of energy, excited
  • Cognitive: positive or optimistic attitude, racing thoughts, impulsiveness 1 2 3

Classification

Bipolar-I Disorder:


  • Occurrence of repeated manic episodes (possible occurrence of depressive phases)

Bipolar-II Disorder:


  • Alternation of hypomania (= mild manic phases) with depressive episodes

Cyclothymic Disorder:


  • Chronic disturbance of mood and drive
  • lasts at least 2 years
  • on more than 50% of days, hypomanic symptoms (not episodes) alternate with depressive states (not episodes) 4

Epidemiology

(= incidence of diseases in the population)

Progression:


  • very different progressions:
    • Few episodes in a lifetime
    • 100 or more episodes in a lifetime
  • High relapse rate 5

Formation Conditions:


  • Genetics:
    • Heredity: 7-10%
    • Heredity of identical twins: match about 60% 6
  • Circadian (= fluctuations in body functions) rythm:
    • Changes in sleep patterns
    • Fluctuations in body temperature and heart rate 7
  • Cellular and nerve-related factors:
    • Altered intracellular signal conversion 8
    • Lack of neurotrophins (= signalling proteins) => brain has limited trainability 9 10
  • Psychological factors:
    • Over-activation (e.g. increased drive) or under-activation (e.g. lack of drive) of the BAS (= behavioural activation system) leads to manic or depressive symptoms 11 12
    • Distorted thinking reinforces extreme mood => thus escalation of manic or depressive episodes (e.g. ignoring negative feedback) 13 14
    • Misinterpretation of internal conditions (e.g. overvaluation of physical sensations, such as fatigue = depression) 15

Additional Info:


  • about 40 million people diagnosed with Bipolar Disorder 16
  • Lifetime prevalence (= cases of illness in a lifetime): 1-5% 17
  • Highest incidence at the age of 15-19
  • Influencing factors: female, life events, existing psychotic symptoms 18
  • Highest suicide risk of all psychiatric disorders 19

Possible Treatment

Drug Therapy:


  • Relapse Prophylactic Therapy with mood stabilizers 20 21 22
  • Treatment of Mania with Antipsychotics (eg. Aripirazole, Olanzapine)
  • Treatment of bipolar depression with both, Antidepressants and mood stabilizers 23 21 24

Psychoeducation and Psychotherapy:


  • In combination with drug treatment
  • especially useful for psychosocial problems (e.g. family conflicts, self-esteem, stress) 25
  • Used Therapies:
    • Family-Focused Treatment (FFT) 26
    • Interpersonal and social rhythm therapy (IPSRT) 27
    • Cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) 28 29
