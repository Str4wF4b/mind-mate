Generalized Anxiety Disorder

What is it?

GAD (Generalized Anxiety Disorder) involves persistent and excessive worry about various life events. The anxiety is often disproportionate to the situation and lasts for an extended period, typically six months or more.

Symptoms

  • Restlessness
  • Difficulty Concentrating
  • Muscle Tension
  • Sleep Disturbances
  • Irritability

Possible Treatment

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy:


  • Helps patients to manage and reframe negative thinking patterns.

Medications:


  • Antidepressants
  • Anti-anxiety Drugs

Relaxation Techniques:


  • Deep Breathing
  • Meditation
  • Progressive Muscle Relaxation1
