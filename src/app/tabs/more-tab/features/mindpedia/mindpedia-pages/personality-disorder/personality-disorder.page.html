Personality Disorder

What is it?

Personality disorders are enduring patterns of behavior and inner experience that deviate markedly from cultural expectations. These can be grouped into categories like borderline, antisocial, or narcissistic personality disorders.

Symptoms

  • Distorted self-image
  • Difficulties in interpersonal relationships
  • Impulsivity
  • Emotional instability

Possible Treatment

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT):


  • Especially used for borderline personality disorder.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy:


  • Used to modify maladaptive behavior patterns.

Long-term Psychotherapy:


  • Used to address deep-seated issues related to personality. 1
