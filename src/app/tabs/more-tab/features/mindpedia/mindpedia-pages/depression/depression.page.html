Depression

What is it?

Depression describes a condition in which symptoms such as sadness, melancholy or listlessness persist for at least two weeks. These can be caused by stressful events, such as diseases or social stress. In addition to a dejected and sad mood, people affected also experience a loss of pleasure and interest in common activities. Major depression can also lead to loss of appetite, sleep disorders, loss of self-esteem and suicidal thoughts or actions.

Symptoms

Emotional Symptoms:


  • Sadness
  • Dejection
  • Anxiety
  • Melancholy

Cognitive Symptoms:


  • Pondering
  • Pessimism
  • Negative thoughts
  • Suicidal thoughts

Physiological-vegetative Symptoms:


  • Lack of energy
  • Tiredness
  • Listlessness
  • Loss of appetite

Behavioral / motoric Symptoms:


  • Slowed speech and motor skills
  • Low activity rate
  • Avoidance of eye contact
  • Suicidal actions

Epidemiology

(= incidence of diseases in the population)

Progression:


  • Major Depression:
    • Variable start: acute or gradual over weeks or months
    • Untreated first episode: 3-4 months until remission 2
    • 1/3 of people affected: only one episode, 1/3: relapsing progression with complete remission, 1/3: chronic and long-term progression without or partial/short-term remission 3
  • Dysthymia:
    • Gradual start
    • Early onset, if before the age of 21
    • Chronic and long-term progression with possible spontaneous remission (10%)
    • Major Depression develops in 75% of cases

Risk Factors:


  • Genetics:
    • 2-4 times increased risk 4
  • Age:
    • Childhood to mid-adulthood: low risk
    • Mid-adulthood to a high adult age: risk increases steadily 2 5
  • Gender:
    • Women twice as high risk as men
  • Marital Status:
    • Divorced, separated, widowed: very high risk
  • Socio-economic (= education, income, occupation) Status:
    • Low socio-economic status: high risk 6 7
  • Geographical Region:
    • City vs. Country: almost equal risk 8 9 7
  • Life Events:
    • Psychosocial stress factors (e.g. death of a loved one, loss incident): high risk 10
    • Early onset, if before the age of 21
  • Comorbidity:
    • Anxiety disorders: 50-60% 11
    • Behavioral, eating, personality and emotional disorders, ADHD: very high risk
    • Physical diseases: high risk 12

Additional Info:


  • 2.4 billion (456 million in Europe 13) people diagnosed with Depression
  • Lifetime prevalence (= cases of illness in a lifetime): 30% Depression, 15-20% Major Depression, 4.5% Dysthymia 14 15
  • Highest incidence at the age of 25-35 5 15
  • Higher risk of suicide and mortality with depressive disease
    • 50% with Major Depression: suicidal thoughts
    • over 10% with Major Depression: suicide attempt 15
    • 15% with Major Depression: death by suicide 3

Possible Treatment

Pharmacological / somatic Therapy:


  • Goal: acute alleviation through to remission
  • Administration of medication such as Antidepressants or Antipsychotics

Psychological Therapies:


  • Behavioral Therapy: treatment and modification of pathological behavior
  • Cognitive Therapy: Detection and modification of negative thoughts and thought processes
  • Duration: about 3-9 months (possibly longer) 16
