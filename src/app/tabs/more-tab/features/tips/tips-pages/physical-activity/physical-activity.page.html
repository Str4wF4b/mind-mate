Physical Activity & Exercise

How it works:

Regular physical activity can improve mood and reduce symptoms of anxiety and depression.

Why it works:

Exercise promotes the release of endorphins, which are often referred to as "feel-good" hormones. It can also increase the availability of neurotransmitters like serotonin, which play a role in mood regulation.

Sharma, A., et al. (2015). Exercise for mental health: A review of the evidence. Journal of Clinical Psychiatry, 76(1), 33-40.
Craft, L. L., & Perna, F. M. (2004). The benefits of exercise for the clinically depressed. Primary Care Companion to the Journal of Clinical Psychiatry, 6(3), 104-111.
